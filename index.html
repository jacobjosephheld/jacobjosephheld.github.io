About

Jacob Joseph Held is a director working in London and Bristol. Jacob has an award winning filmography of short dramas and documentaries, and also directs for the stage.

Jacob's short films have explored themes of boyhood, magical realism, loss and grief. Notably, Reverie (2021) was a part of an exclusive BFI showcase and his most recent film Puddle Man (2025) has been nominated for a RTS award.

Jacob's work in documentaries have included Six Weeks (2024), an experimental documentary following a case of teenage cancer, making the argument for creativity as a healer. Six Weeks has been shown in festivals across the UK and is now used in medical research at University College Hospital. Jacob's recent documentaries include Hunted (2025), an expose of illegal fox hunting in Somerset, and Gullible (2026), a comedy mockumentary about seagulls in Western-super-Mare.

This year, Jacob is directing two plays. The first, Fear and Misery in the Third Reich (1938) by Bertolt Brecht, will be coming to the Pegg Theatre in Bristol this March. In August, Jacob will be taking a modern adaptation, Fear and Misery in America, to the Edinburgh Fringe Festival from 16th-22nd at Green Side, George Street.

Although Jacob is at the beginning of his career he has already worked with muli-award winning production companies such as Moonage Pictures, Mammoth Screen, Plimsol Productions and Spun Gold.
